Dear Students, Educators, Families, and Community,

Dear Students, Families, and Staff,

As we begin the 2025–2026 school year, I want to extend a heartfelt welcome to each of you. Whether you’re starting your very first day of school or returning to a familiar campus, this moment is filled with excitement, opportunity, and possibility. It is a true honor to share this journey with you.

Coming home to the Clark County School District, where my path as an educator first began 35 years ago, has been both personally meaningful and inspiring. This District shaped me as a teacher, a leader, and a person. I carry that gratitude with me every day. Now, I feel a renewed sense of purpose and a strong belief in what we can accomplish together.

We know families have many choices when it comes to education, and we are truly grateful you are a member of the CCSD community. This year, our focus is clear. We will restore trust, lead with stability, and empower every student to rise as high as their imaginations will carry them. We do this by showing up with consistency, listening with care, and ensuring your students feel seen, supported, and inspired.

We are fortunate to have dedicated and passionate teachers and staff who are the heart of this District. Their daily commitment to your child’s well-being shapes not only academic success but also confidence and character. As families, I encourage you to build strong connections with your child’s teachers. Open communication, mutual respect, and shared goals create a safe learning environment where every student can thrive. Your involvement in their lives and education makes a lasting impact.

To our students: Know you are the reason we do this work. Your dreams, your voices, and your ideas matter. You belong here, and your potential is limitless.

To our families: Know that your partnership is essential. The support you provide at home, your presence in schools and events, and your advocacy for your child’s education make a meaningful difference in our schools and across the District.

This school year, let’s recommit to what matters most. Let’s lift up every child and unite as a community by building stronger relationships that support students and families. I believe in our shared ability to make this a year filled with connection, growth, and meaningful progress.

With gratitude and excitement,

Jhone Ebert
Superintendent, Clark County School District
